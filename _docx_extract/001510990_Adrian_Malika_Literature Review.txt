Introduction 
Farming & Engineering Services Ltd (FES) is Malawi’s leading provider of integrated agricultural solutions, established in 1967. FES operates a wide range of services which may include equipment selling, maintenance and rental of agricultural machinery and equipment. They inventory includes harvesters, tractors, planters, irrigation systems and many other machineries designed to help improve agricultural productivity (Africa, 2024). It has branches and other service depots located across the country. FES becoming a big partner for farmers seeking to access farming tech without having the high cost of ownership. However, despite FES having a strong operational presence, the rental process still faces a number of challenges due to outdated bookings, tracking and payment system.
Problem Statement
The equipment booking and management system is an online application that is created to facilitate the arrangement of machinery and operators scheduling, allocation, and tracking. Kising’u (2024) asserts that equipment management systems assist organizations to better service delivery through centralizing resources available and automate coordination between customers, machines, and operators. These systems save on administrative resources, eliminate the possibility of a patient being booked twice and allow real-time communication, which are important concerns in the industries where the timeliness of equipment deployment is a major issue. Davison, Goodspeed and Fluharty (2019) also reveal that equipment management systems are important to organizations with large and diverse fleet of machines. Their research work on the Maintenance Equipment Management System (MEMS) outlines four core modules needed to manage equipment well, namely, equipment inventory, maintenance and scheduling, parts and supplies management, and financial analysis tools. These components are integrated to reduce the downtime, maximize resources, increase the life cycle of equipment and enhance accountability. Another issue that the authors bring to attention is that manual processes may create a lot of inefficiencies including incomplete records, weak scheduling habits, slow maintenance and unreliable budgeting. In the absence of a digital system, organizations experience difficulties in monitoring the use of equipment, repairing them, and operational costs.
Importance of an Equipment Booking & Management System
Equipment booking and management system is the key to ensuring that there is efficient use of machine, it has to be deployed on time and it has to enhance coordination within organisations that have machine-based operation. There are a few important reasons why such systems are important:
Better Operational Performance
The management systems of digital equipment make the processes simplified through centralisation of information about bookings, equipment availability, operator shifts, and records of equipment usage. (Kising’u, 2025) cites this centralization as a source of decreased workload in the office, eases the coordinating process, and eradicates typical manual errors, including duplication and loss of records. Automated workflows assist organisations to perform their tasks more efficiently and mainly when the demand level is high.
Less Downtime and Greater Maintenance Scheduling
Davison, Goodspeed and Fluharty (2019) underscores that well-integrated systems help in the effective upkeep planning by availing the precise and up-to-date information regarding the state of the equipment, its history of service and repairs. As their MEMS framework shows, the modules like equipment inventory, maintenance scheduling, and parts tracking allow significantly decreasing downtime, increasing equipment life, and carrying out repairs on time. Lack of digital systems means that organisations will face delays, incompleteness of records, and inconsistent maintenance practices(Gusti and Suryantara, 2019).
Live Monitoring and Additional Micro-location
The current equipment management systems have the use of GPS and Internet of things (IoT) tracking technologies. According to a study by Moumen et al. (2023) real-time monitoring is a way that allows the organisations to monitor the equipment location, usage patterns, and movement in real-time. This minimizes the theft, misuse and inaccessibility risks. Fleet allocation decisions also become easier with real-time visibility provided because it allows matching equipment to operators according to spatiality and availability.
Cost Control and financial accurateness
Automated systems are used to assist organisations to keep proper financial records and cost estimates regarding the equipment rental and usage.  suggest that the accuracy of billing, financial discrepancy, and improved budgeting are always realised in organisations with digital tracking tools. This is particularly relevant in the case of rental-based services where the customers require transparency and predictability in cost(Gichuki and Mulu-Mutuku, 2018).
Better Customer Experience and Openness.
Equipment booking systems that are offered through the internet enhance customer satisfaction because the client is able to book machinery remotely, get an automatic update, and track the progress of the service. According to(Budhewar et al., 2024), accessibility, less frustration in customers, and timely communication are areas Digital booking platforms enhance to build trust between customers and service providers.

Evaluation Criteria 
Navigation
EquipmentShare is well-structured and its navigation is arranged by a well-designed top navigation menu which gives direct access to key areas of core systems like Rent, Buy, Service, Technology, and Company information. The menus are divided into groups in a logical manner and a few clicks are made to access important features. This hierarchical navigation enhances efficiency and removes frustration to the user, especially to a first-time user. 
Accessibility
EquipmentShare is accessible on both desktop and mobile platforms, allowing users to manage equipment remotely. However, the system relies heavily on stable internet connectivity, which may pose challenges in rural farming areas such as those served by FES.
 
Appearance 
The visuals displayed by EquipmentShare interface is good and professional in its system layout. The overall bold typography and the use of a very limited palette of colours, which consists of dark colours and contrasting orange action buttons, contribute to the better readability and focus to the most important functions. This visual consistency assists in creating brand name acceptance and a good look that is suitable for the professional equipment rental and management setup.
